I am a 4th-year Computer Science undergraduate at the University of California, Santa Barbara within the College of Creative Studies. I am currently a student researcher in the UCSB Neural Information Retrieval Lab, advised by Prof. Tao Yang.

I am applying to Ph.D. programs in Computer Science for the 2026 academic year.

My research focuses on the intersection of Systems and Machine Learning. I am interested in designing the data infrastructure and retrieval logic required to support reliable, large-scale Agentic AI systems.

I am currently investigating efficient hybrid retrieval architectures: combining dense semantic embeddings with learned sparse representations to improve search quality without sacrificing efficiency. My daily work involves open-source vector search libraries like FAISS and experimenting with dynamic pruning strategies on the MS MARCO benchmark. I am broadly interested in how we can co-design retrieval algorithms with low-level systems infrastructure to handle the latency constraints of modern agents.

Previously, I interned at NetApp, where I built agents and RAG pipelines for root cause analysis on system logs. Before that, I worked at Inogen on medical device simulation, app development, and firmware testing. Beyond my technical work, I founded the UCSB ACM Student Chapter. Watching this community take root and learning from the incredible students, faculty, and staff at UCSB has been a highlight of my time here.

If you are interested in discussing efficient retrieval, AI systems, or distributed infrastructure, feel free to reach out!
